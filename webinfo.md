3. Why do many people study English for years but still struggle to communicate?

I want this section to explain that the problem isn't the student.

Most people simply don't have enough opportunities to use English in real life.

4. Why English Homestay?

Instead of comparing ourselves with other schools, I'd like to explain our philosophy through storytelling.

For example:

"If you want to learn swimming, would you only read a book, or would you get into the water?"

English works the same way.

5. Our Learning Method

Our philosophy is simple:

30% learning

70% practicing

Students learn the fundamentals first, then immediately apply them through real conversations.

6. Learning Journey

Explain our learning process.

The first 4–5 lessons help students understand English pronunciation rules and become confident reading new words.

Then we gradually move into communication, listening, asking questions, and speaking naturally.

7. A Week at English Homestay

This is one of the most important sections.

Students have:

Two main classes every week

Each class lasts 1.5 hours.

60 minutes with a qualified Vietnamese teacher.
30 minutes practicing with international volunteers.
Free Talk Sessions

From Monday to Saturday, mainly between 6:30 PM and 10:00 PM.

Students can join whenever it suits their schedule.

They may stay for:

30 minutes
1 hour
2 hours
or even the whole evening.

During this time, teaching assistants and international volunteers are available to support conversations.

180 Conversation Topics

Many students don't know what to talk about.

We've prepared more than 180 conversation topics, so every practice session has a clear purpose.

Beyond the classroom

After class, students often:

Go for coffee
Have dinner together
Play board games
Join weekend activities

This is where English becomes part of everyday life.
